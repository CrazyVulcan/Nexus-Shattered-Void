2025-06-15 · Announcement

Website Launch & Digital Tools Now Live

We're thrilled to announce the launch of the official Nexus: Shattered Void website! The site includes the full core rulebook (readable online, downloadable PDF coming soon), the interactive Ship Builder, the Factions Database, and the Lore Archive.

The Ship Builder is our flagship digital tool. It allows you to design custom fleet lists for any faction, equip weapons and modules within slot limits, track point costs in real time, and export or share builds with a link. Fleet saves are stored locally in your browser—no account required.

We'll be rolling out new features over the coming months, including the Battle Simulator, community fleet gallery, and online multiplayer. Stay tuned.

2025-06-08 · Rulebook Update

Core Rules v1.0 — Balance Notes

After six months of playtesting with our community group, we've finalized the Core Rules at v1.0. Here's a summary of the major changes from the playtest draft:

  • Priority system revised: Priority now uses highest Sensors in fleet (previously highest Speed). This makes sensor-heavy builds like the Chorus Collective more viable in initiative.
  • Crippled threshold changed: Ships become Crippled at 50% HP instead of 33%. Crippled penalty reduced from −2 to −1 attack. Early feedback found the old system punished slow ships too harshly.
  • Iron Dominion Unyielding: Changed from "ignore first 2 damage per round" to "ignore first 1 damage per round." Was too powerful in combination with Ablative Armor.
  • Void Syndicate Scavenge: Trigger now requires the destroying ship to be within 6" of the wreck. Previously had no range restriction, which created edge cases.

Full changelog available on the GitHub repository.

2025-05-20 · Community

Playtest Recap: Operation Shattered Cordon

Last month we ran our largest organized playtest yet: Operation Shattered Cordon, an eight-player, four-faction multiplayer scenario using the Breakthrough and Nexus Fragment Capture scenarios back-to-back.

The results were fascinating. The Void Syndicate dominated the Breakthrough scenario (as expected—their speed advantage in open scenarios is significant), but struggled badly in the Fragment Capture scenario, where durable ships that can hold positions shine.

The Iron Dominion performed exactly as designed in Fragment Capture: their Conquest-class Dreadnought locked down one objective zone entirely for four rounds, forcing opponents to play around it. However, the 280-point cost left the rest of the fleet thin, and a coordinated Terran Republic assault overwhelmed their supporting frigates.

The Chorus Collective's Bio-Regeneration rule proved critical in the endgame—two Bloom-class Carriers that should have been destroyed survived to turn the tide. We're watching that one closely.

Thank you to all twenty-three playtesters who participated. Your feedback directly shapes the final rules.

2025-04-10 · Development

Battle Simulator — Development Preview

We're in early development on the Battle Simulator—a browser-based tool that will let you simulate fleet engagements without needing a physical table. Here's what's planned for the first release:

  • Import fleets from the Ship Builder
  • Automated dice resolution with modifier handling
  • Turn-by-turn combat log
  • Support for all four core factions and their special rules
  • Basic scenario support (Total Annihilation first)

Multiplayer (shared link sessions) is planned for a later release. Expected early access: Q3 2025.
