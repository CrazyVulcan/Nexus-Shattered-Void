What is Nexus: Shattered Void?

A tactical starship combat wargame centered on subsystem warfare, power allocation, and progressive capability loss.

🎯

SSD-Centered Design

Each ship is represented by a Ship System Display — a physical card that is your primary play surface. The SSD tracks live damage state across all subsystems. It is not a reference sheet. It is the game.

Read Rules →
⚡

Power Pressure

Ships run on a power budget. Engineering damage shrinks that budget permanently. Do you fire your primary weapon this turn, or save power for shields? Decisions compound as the engineering track degrades: 4→3→2→1.

Damage That Changes What You Can Do

Hits go to subsystems, not to an abstract health pool. Every track that degrades costs you capability.

🎲

d6 Hit Allocation

When a ship is hit, roll 1d6 to determine which subsystem absorbs the damage. The SSD shows which values map to which track.

🔧

Engineering Degradation

As engineering boxes are checked off, the ship crosses power thresholds. Maximum available power drops permanently over the course of the battle.

🚀

FTL Degradation

The FTL drive has its own track. Damage it and the ship's ability to jump out — or in — deteriorates. Cutting off escape is a viable tactical goal.

💥

Weapon Degradation

Weapons degrade step-by-step instead of flipping from active to destroyed. A battle-worn weapon fires less dice, costs more power, or loses range before it finally goes dark.
